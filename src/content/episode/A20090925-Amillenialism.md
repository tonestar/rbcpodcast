A thorough theological treatment of a kingdom already inaugurated in Christ's ministry, death, and resurrection, yet still awaiting its consummation at his return. Riddlebarger engages the history of interpretation and argues for an inaugurated eschatology that maintains the New Testament's tension between present kingdom reality and future kingdom fullness — without collapsing into overrealized triumphalism.
