Riddlebarger turns to the "unholy trinity" of Revelation 12–13 — the dragon (Satan), the beast from the sea, and the beast from the earth (the false prophet). He interprets these figures within their first-century Roman imperial context while demonstrating their ongoing typological significance for the church in every age. John's vision of satanic opposition to the church is not confined to one historical moment but describes the character of antichristian power throughout history.
