A survey of the core eschatological themes across the New Testament. Riddlebarger examines how Paul, the author of Hebrews, and the Gospel writers all share a common eschatological framework in which the decisive age-turning event is the death, resurrection, and exaltation of Jesus Christ. The second coming, final judgment, and resurrection are the completion of what the first advent inaugurated.
