Focusing on the signs that immediately precede Christ's coming, Riddlebarger examines the conversion of Israel (Romans 11), the final apostasy, and the great tribulation that signals the end of the age. He argues that Reformed amillennialism does not flatten out these signs but rather takes them seriously as real events that will be visible to the church — events that should produce not fear but confidence in God's sovereign governance of history toward its appointed consummation.
