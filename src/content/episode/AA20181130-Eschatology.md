Continuing the examination of Reformation-era eschatology, this second lecture goes deeper into the writings of Calvin, Bullinger, and the Westminster divines on the millennium, the resurrection, and the final judgment. Riddlebarger also traces how post-Reformation orthodoxy maintained a two-age eschatological framework and how that inheritance was later disrupted by nineteenth-century prophetic novelties. Essential listening for understanding where Reformed eschatology comes from and why it remains compelling.
