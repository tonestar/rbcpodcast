A focused exegesis of 2 Thessalonians 2 and its "man of lawlessness." Riddlebarger surveys the major historical candidates — Nero, the Roman emperors, the papacy, and a future individual — and evaluates each against the text. He argues that the Reformed and Reformational identification with the papacy has serious exegetical warrant, while also showing how the man of lawlessness points to a final eschatological embodiment of opposition to Christ before his return.
