The third session devoted to the Antichrist figure draws together threads from the Olivet Discourse, Revelation 13, and Daniel 7–8 to show how the biblical writers present variations on a single eschatological theme: a beast-like power that persecutes the church, demands worship, and deceives the nations. Riddlebarger shows that while this pattern has been embodied historically in various empires and rulers, the New Testament anticipates a final and climactic manifestation immediately before the return of Christ.
