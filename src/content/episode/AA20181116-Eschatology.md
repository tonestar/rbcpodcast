The first of two lectures examining what the Protestant Reformers themselves believed about last things. Riddlebarger surveys the eschatological outlook of Luther, Calvin, Zwingli, and the Reformed confessions, demonstrating that their views were substantially amillennial and bore little resemblance to the nineteenth-century dispensationalism so prevalent in contemporary evangelicalism. He shows how the Reformers read Revelation, Daniel, and the Olivet Discourse within a strong redemptive-historical framework.
