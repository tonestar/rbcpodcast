A continuation of the study of the Antichrist figure, focusing on 2 Thessalonians 2 and the enigmatic "restrainer." Riddlebarger examines Paul's description of the man of sin's self-exaltation in the temple and the apostasy that precedes his revelation. He addresses the major interpretive options for the restrainer — the Roman empire, the Holy Spirit, the preaching of the gospel — and shows how the passage fits within the broader New Testament eschatological framework of the present age as a time of both gospel advance and intensifying opposition.
