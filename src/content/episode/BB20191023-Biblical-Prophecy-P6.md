A continuation of the previous study on covenant and eschatology, this lecture examines the land promises, the Davidic dynasty, and the prophetic expectation of a new Jerusalem in light of their New Testament fulfilment. Riddlebarger engages the dispensationalist insistence on a literal, future fulfilment of land promises to ethnic Israel and responds with an exegetical and theological case that these promises find their fullest expression in Christ, the heavenly Jerusalem, and the new creation.
