This lecture develops the biblical-theological framework of "this age" and "the age to come" — what scholars call the two-age model. Riddlebarger argues that this structure, rooted in Jewish apocalyptic hope and decisively transformed by Christ's resurrection, provides the essential grid for understanding New Testament eschatology. The overlapping of the ages, inaugurated at Pentecost and consummated at the parousia, explains why the New Testament is simultaneously "already" and "not yet" in its eschatological outlook.
